# VISION: Tavli Digital Café

## The "Neo-Café" Concept
Tavli Digital Café is not just another backgammon site; it is a **digital village square**. Unlike clinical, Western-centric platforms that prioritize cold efficiency and ELO ratings above all else, our platform recreates the social atmosphere of a traditional Mediterranean café (Kafenio, Kahvehane). 

It is a place where the sound of dice hitting wood, the rhythmic movement of checkers, and the banter between players are as important as the game itself. We focus on the regional variants that define the culture of the Mediterranean—**Portes**, **Plakoto**, and **Fevga**—bringing the authentic "Tavli" experience to the digital age.

## Target Audience
Our primary audience resides in **Greece, Turkey, and the Middle East**, as well as the global diaspora from these regions. For these communities, Tavli is more than a game; it is a cultural touchstone, a social lubricant, and a lifelong pastime. We aim to honor this heritage while providing modern, reliable technology.

## Core Principles

### 1. Mobile-First PWA
The platform is designed as a Progressive Web App (PWA) from day one. It must be fast, installable on a home screen, and perform flawlessly on mobile devices, reflecting how our users interact with the web today.

### 2. Frictionless Entry
Joining a game should be as easy as pulling up a chair at a café. We prioritize low-friction onboarding, allowing users to spectate or join matches with minimal barriers.

### 3. Provably Fair & Privacy-First (Event Sourcing)
Trust is the foundation of the Digital Café. By utilizing an **Event Sourcing** architecture, every action in the game—from dice rolls to checker moves—is recorded as an immutable event. This ensures complete transparency, auditability, and a "provably fair" environment where the history of every match is indisputable.

### 4. Tactile UI/UX
The digital experience must feel physical. We emphasize high-quality animations, haptic feedback (where available), and sound design that captures the satisfying "clack" of checkers. The UI should evoke the warmth of wood and the atmosphere of a sun-drenched terrace.
